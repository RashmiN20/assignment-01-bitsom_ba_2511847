## Vector DB Use Case

A law firm handling 500‑page contracts needs more than a traditional keyword search. While keyword search can quickly locate exact terms, it fails when lawyers phrase queries in plain English or when contracts use varied terminology. For example, a lawyer asking *“What are the termination clauses?”* might miss sections written as *“grounds for ending the agreement”* or *“conditions for cancellation.”* Legal documents are dense, complex, and often inconsistent in wording, so keyword search alone risks overlooking critical clauses.

- **Limitations of keyword search**  
  - Only matches exact words or phrases.  
  - Cannot capture synonyms, paraphrasing, or contextual meaning.  
  - Produces either too many irrelevant hits or misses important sections.  

- **Role of vector databases**  
  - Store embeddings that represent the semantic meaning of text.  
  - Convert a lawyer’s natural language query into an embedding and compare it against embeddings of contract sections.  
  - Retrieve passages that are semantically similar, even if the wording differs.  
  - Example: A query about *termination clauses* could surface sections on *contract cancellation* or *agreement dissolution.*  

- **Benefits for the law firm**  
  - Enables natural language search across lengthy contracts.  
  - Improves efficiency by reducing manual scanning.  
  - Ensures lawyers don’t miss critical clauses due to wording differences.  
  - Acts as a semantic search engine bridging plain English queries with technical legal language.  

In short, a vector database transforms contract review from keyword matching into meaning‑based retrieval, making it far more effective for legal professionals.
